# What to prepare before hiring a technical consultant

A technical consultant can help much faster when the business context is clear. You do not need a complete specification, but you do need enough information to make the conversation concrete.

## Bring the business goal

Explain what should improve: faster reporting, fewer manual steps, better customer onboarding, clearer operations, a new digital service, or a more reliable internal system.

Good technical decisions depend on knowing what the company is trying to achieve.

## Bring the current workflow

Share the tools, files, people, and steps involved today. Screenshots, sample documents, anonymized data, and short process notes are often more useful than a long feature list.

## Bring constraints

Timeline, budget range, internal capacity, security needs, data sensitivity, and existing systems all shape the right recommendation.

Constraints are not obstacles. They are what make a technical plan realistic.

## Ask for a decision, not just an opinion

The best outcome from early consulting is a decision: build or buy, automate or simplify, prototype or wait, use one stack or another, start now or prepare more.

Clear input makes that decision easier to reach.
