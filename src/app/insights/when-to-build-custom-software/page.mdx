# When should a business build custom software?

Custom software is rarely the first answer. A good SaaS tool, a spreadsheet, or a lighter automation can be the better business decision when the workflow is simple and the cost of change is low.

The case for custom software becomes stronger when the current process is important, repeated, hard to control, and connected to revenue, delivery quality, compliance, or customer experience.

## Useful signals

- The same information is copied between multiple tools every week.
- Reports are assembled manually and nobody fully trusts the numbers.
- The team has created workarounds that only one or two people understand.
- SaaS tools cover 70 percent of the workflow but the missing 30 percent creates daily friction.
- The business process is a differentiator, not just administration.

## Start smaller than you think

The safest first step is not a huge platform. It is usually a focused internal tool, dashboard, workflow, or integration that removes one painful bottleneck.

That first version should prove three things: the workflow is understood, the users will actually use it, and the technical foundation can grow without a rewrite.

## Good first questions

- Who uses the process today?
- What breaks most often?
- Which manual step costs the most time or risk?
- What data needs to be trusted?
- What would make the team say, "this is already useful"?

Custom software is worth considering when it gives the company better control over work that already matters.
